# Decision memo template

For non-trivial product decisions: kill / continue / pivot, build vs. buy, refactor vs. rebuild, pricing changes, scope expansion.

Designed to be short. A two-page decision memo beats a 15-slide deck for most decisions.

---

## Title

[Short, specific. The decision in one line.]

## Recommendation

One sentence, owned. *I recommend we [decision], for [primary reason].*

Take a position. Hedging is not a recommendation.

## Context

What changed that makes this decision necessary now? (2–4 sentences.)

## The decision

What are we deciding *between*? List the real options. Not "do nothing" as a default — describe what "do nothing" actually means.

- **Option A:** [...]
- **Option B:** [...]
- **Option C:** [...]

## Reversibility

Is this a one-way door or two-way door?

- If reversible: what does the unwind cost?
- If irreversible: what's the rigor we've applied to match the irreversibility?

For one-way doors, the rest of this memo deserves more weight than for two-way doors.

## Tradeoffs

Two or three most important tradeoffs across the options. Honest comparison.

For each tradeoff:
- What's gained
- What's lost
- Which option does best on this dimension

## Load-bearing assumptions

What would have to be true for the recommendation to be right?

- Assumption 1 — evidence / hypothesis / guess?
- Assumption 2 — evidence / hypothesis / guess?
- Assumption 3 — evidence / hypothesis / guess?

If two or more are guesses, the memo is not yet ready to commit.

## Risks and mitigations

Three risks max. The ones that matter most.

For each:
- The risk
- The signal that would tell us it's materializing
- The mitigation

## Success and failure criteria

Pre-committed.

- **Success:** [metric, value, timeline]
- **Failure:** [metric, threshold, timeline]
- **Kill criterion:** what would force us to stop?

## Asks

If the recommendation requires support from leadership, name it specifically:

- Headcount: [n people, roles]
- Air cover: [specific narrative or commitment]
- Other: [specific resource or constraint relief]

Vague support is not actionable.

## Open questions

What's still unresolved. For each: owner, deadline.

---

## How to use

Write the recommendation *first*. Then write the rest as the defense of the recommendation.

If, while writing the rest, the recommendation visibly breaks — change the recommendation. The memo is a forcing function for clarity, not a sales document for a decision you've already made.
